Auto racing in the 1970s considered trophy girls part of the competition. They were in the victory lane, in front of pace cars, and interacted with pit crews. Furthermore, they never considered wearing bras, and their hair grew longer. One trophy girl that stood out during this time was Barbara Roufs.

Barbara Roufs was an American trophy girl and model. She was well known for her stunning looks, including broad straightened hair and 60s gogo boots. Although drag racing had many trophy girls, Barbara became famous because she was one of the oldest at 29.

She had an outstanding physique that made her a favourite of many companies that used her for marketing their products, particularly clothing items. Unfortunately, she died in 1991. However, drag racing enthusiasts still remember her to this day.
