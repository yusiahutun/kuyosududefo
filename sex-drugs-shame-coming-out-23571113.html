Sex and drugs shame, coming out and Netflix hits - what One Foot in the Grave stars did next

Publish date: 2024-02-11

Grumpy Victor Meldrew grew ever more furious as he landed in increasingly unlikely situations in One Foot in the Grave.

Bellowing out his catchphrase, “I don’t believe it!” he became one of the most recognisable characters on British TV during the show’s decade-long run between 1990 and 2000.

He along with the show's other main cast members has enjoyed a successful acting career since it ended.

Wilson appeared in plenty of other shows after One Foot in The Grave, most notably Merlin opposite Colin Morgan as the fabled wizard.

Now 84, the Scots actor came out as gay in 2013. He is also an accomplished theatre director and godfather to co-star Angus Deayton’s son.

Annette Crosbie (Margaret Meldrew)

Scottish actress Crosbie, 87, was Victor’s put-upon wife.

She has since been in films including the 2016 Dad’s Army remake and Hollywood musical into The Woods, and TV shows such as Dial M for Middlesbrough and Call The Midwife.

Last year she appeared in an episode of Ricky Gervais’ hit Netflix show After Life. The mum-of-two is divorced and her actress daughter Selina played alcoholic Pauline in Benidorm.

Angus Deayton (Patrick Trench)

At the same time as playing Victor’s long-suffering neighbour, Deayton hosted Have I Got News For You until 2002 when he got the boot over a sex and drugs scandal.

He then starred in dark comedy Nighty Night and hosted Would I Lie To You? and Hell’s Kitchen, where he was axed after arguing with chef Marco Pierre White.

In 2012 he was cynical teacher George Windsor in Waterloo Road and has also popped up in Death in Paradise and Benidorm.

Deayton, 65, has one son.

Janine Duvitski (Pippa Trench)

She appeared in many popular shows and was Jacqueline stewart in Benidorm from 2007 to 2018.

She played Elsie in the 2016 Still Open All Hours Christmas special and was in Hugh Grant film About A Boy.

Janine, 68, married in 1983 and has four children, including Ruby Bentall who played Verity in the BBC’s Poldark series.
