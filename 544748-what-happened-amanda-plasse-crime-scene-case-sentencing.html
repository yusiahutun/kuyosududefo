Who was Amanda Plasse?

Amanda Plasse was an American waiter and hostess known for her love for art, journaling, poetry and music. She was born on 11 December 1990 in Chicopee, Massachusetts, USA and died on 26 August 2011, aged 20, in the same city. Her mother's name is Michelle Mathieson. She had two siblings, Amieelee and Nicholas.

She was also an employee of Friendly's restaurant on Memorial Drive, Chicopee, dating Seth Green, a carpenter. Amanda Plasse and Seth Green's relationship was only one week old at her demise.

Amanda Plasse's death

On 26 August 2011, Amanda Plasse's boyfriend, Seth Green, discovered her dead body inside the kitchen of her third-floor apartment at 73 School Street in Chicopee, Massachusetts. He dialled 911, with the police arriving at the scene at approximately 6:50 pm. At the scene, police discovered Amanda's body with multiple stab wounds.

According to the police report, notable observations of the scene included bloodstains all over the kitchen, which signified a violent struggle between the killer and Amanda. Police also noticed dry blood-stained shoe prints, notably Nike Air Max with a non-marking sole on the kitchen floor.

The shoe prints, determined to be either women's US size 9.5 or men's size 7½, helped the investigators to determine the time of the murder as between 4:10 pm and 5 pm.

The detectives also found a broken window pane from inside the apartment, a palm print on the window and large amounts of DNA under her fingernails. According to the autopsy report, Amanda's cause of death was due to injuries to her vital organs due to stab wounds.

Amanda Plasse's murder investigation

Due to the nature of the crime, which police termed as a crime of passion, their initial suspect was the boyfriend, Seth Green. However, the results were negative after further investigation and tests, including a polygraph test, palm prints, shoe size comparisons, and DNA matching. They cleared him from the suspect list.

They followed other leads, including Amanda's previous boyfriends and best friend. However, they all led to dead ends. In 2013, detectives decided to review the evidence with a fresh perspective. To their surprise, they discovered a new piece of evidence. A message was hidden in plain sight on a dry-erase whiteboard in Amanda's bedroom.

Per the police report, the message read,

Dennis was here 8/11/11.

The date on the whiteboard was 15 days before Amanda's murder in her apartment. Police also discovered through a 2011 recorded interview with Amanda's former boyfriend that she had confronted a person who had broken into her apartment.

Amanda's former boyfriend recalled the alleged burglar's name, beginning with the letter D, and added that he was a regular around the area. After detectives ran a check, they came up with two individuals named Dennis, who lived near 73 School Street in 2011.

After further cross-referencing, police found Dennis Rosa-Roman, who had lived three blocks away. Police called him in for an interview in November 2013. Detectives noticed his shoes matched the 2011 crime scene print. Dennis' DNA sample also matched the one found under Amanda's nails.

On 5 November 2013, Dennis Rosa-Roman was arrested and charged with murdering Amanda Plasse.

Amanda Plasse's murder trial and sentencing

On 7 July 2016, five years after the murder, Dennis went to trial and was convicted of first-degree murder after an 8-day trial concluded. The judge handed him a life sentence without the chance of parole. According to various reports, Dennis is likely serving jail time in a prison in Massachusetts.

What is Amanda's law in Massachusetts?

Amanda's law makes it a criminal offence for first responders, including police officers, to capture photos of victims of crimes, accidents or emergencies unless as part of the official duties or with the victim's consent. The law also makes the offence punishable by a year in jail or a $2,000 fine.

The law occurred after two Chicopee Police officers took photographs of Amanda's crime scene with their cell phones. They then sent these pictures to fellow officers, and one even showed photographs to coaches at a youth football game.
