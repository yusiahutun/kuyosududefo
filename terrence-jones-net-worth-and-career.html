How did Terrence Jones start his Professional Career?

Terrence attended Thomas Jefferson High School in Portland, Oregon, where he played high school basketball. He had attended that school as a child and advanced to the high school basketball team.

He thrived in the power forward position from the start and performed admirably. His enormous stature proved to be a huge advantage in his dominance of the game, and he had outstanding high school records. One of his many contributions was leading Jefferson to three consecutive Oregon class 5A state championships.

His junior and senior seasons showed a significant improvement. As a result, he received numerous honors, including selection to the Junior National Select Team, which competed in the 2010 Nike Hoop Summit.

In both of his previous high school seasons, he averaged more than 30.0 points and 10.0 points per game. In addition, he was named to the McDonald’s All-American Game team in 2010.

Jones was selected by the Houston Rockets in the eighth round of the 2012 NBA Draft. That year’s draft was the most memorable for the Wildcats, as six of their players were drafted and went on to play in the NBA.

Terrence played in the NBA Development League after being recruited and quickly became one of its top performers. However, as a rookie, he did not see much action.

He appeared in only 19 regular-season games, averaging 5.5 points and 3.7 rebounds per game. In the second season, he was given more matches by the club. Terrence started 71 of the 74 games he played in that season, averaging 12.1 points per game.

He returned to the Rockets for two more seasons, but his numbers were dismal. In addition, he sustained several injuries, including a concussion and an eye injury, which forced him to miss many matches. To make matters worse, he was involved in an accident and was hospitalized for several days.

In 2016, the Rockets traded him to the New Orleans Pelicans. He showed some signs of improvement during the 2016-17 season, but he was eventually traded to the Milwaukee Bucks the following season.

He briefly played for the Houston Rockets again in the 2018-19 season after returning from the Chinese Basketball League. Jones also appeared in 2017 for the Chinese Basketball Association (CBA) club Qingdao Eagles.

Furthermore, in 2019, he played for the TNT KaTropa in the Philippine Basketball Association (PBA). He has also participated in a few other foreign leagues. Terrence recently joined the Grand Rapids Gold, a Denver Nuggets affiliate team, to play in the NBA G League.
