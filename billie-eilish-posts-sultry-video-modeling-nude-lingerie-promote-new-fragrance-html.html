Billie Eilish posts sultry video modeling nude lingerie to promote new fragrance

Publish date: 2024-04-08

Billie Eilish posted a sultry video of her modeling nude lingerie on Friday, telling her 108 million Instagram followers to buy her fragrance for Valentine's Day.

'Shop Eilish for Valentine's Day,' captioned the 21-year-old music sensation, who recently filed a restraining order against her alleged stalker.

Billie posed seductively in the blurry footage, which saw her cradling a bottle of Eilish perfume as the camera scanned over her legs, cleavage and face.

The pop star previously sent Instagram ablaze in November when she posted images of herself getting drenched in a dark downpour of rain to promote the launch of her sophomore fragrance, Eilish No. 2.

Captioning the show-stopping images, Billie wrote: 'I couldn’t be happier with every piece of this. I’m beyond excited for you to have it.'

Eilish explained in a statement that she 'really liked the idea of a darker, rainy world for Eilish No. 2' and that her latest fragrance conjures a 'sultry and wet feeling.'

Elements of 'warmth and sweetness' combine with 'spicy and woodsy' notes to convey the Lovely singer's 'sensual side.'   

Vegan, cruelty-free and made with clean ingredients, Eilish reportedly brings her fragrances to life by going to the fragrance labs and working with the head perfumer herself. 

After the 'worldwide success' of the singer's first fragrance in 2021 – a vanilla-forward scent – it was only a matter of time before Billie's sophomore perfume rained down upon the marketplace.

In an interview with Vogue, Billie gushed about her passion for perfumes, calling them 'one of the greatest joys' in her life. 

She began with vanilla because she's 'always been in love' with it since childhood and because vanilla 'is such a...a loved smell.'

'I have a really strong nose,' explained the burgeoning perfume mogul, 'and since I was like a little kid, smells are all I think about. I associate everything through my nose – like for real.'

Billie's latest Instagram post comes on the heels of her requesting a restraining order against a man she claims tried to break into her family's Los Angeles home.

Last month, a 39-year-old man named Christopher Anderson tried to break into the Highland Park home by jumping a fence, and he was then taken into custody.

The singer also alleged in the order (via TMZ) that Anderson tried to visit her family  home multiple times in December, with local police filing several reports.

The court documents ask for protection for the singer, her 25-year-old brother Finneas and their parents, actress/teacher Maggie Baird and actor Patrick O'Connell.

Billie's father revealed that Anderson has visited their home many times since late December and that he would often profess his love for the Bad Guy singer while hoping to meet her face-to-face. 

The singer claimed these incidents caused her, 'substantial anxiety, fear, and emotional distress,' so much that she doesn't feel safe visiting her parents' home anymore.

Police were called to her parents' Highland Park home on January 5, responding to a call that someone was trying to scale the fence to get into the property.

The masked intruder was was apprehended one block away from the home in a sweep of the area.

The Grammy winner opened up to V magazine last year about handling massive fame at such a young age, calling it 'a bit jarring' for those who knew her growing up.

Eilish went on to explain that her home life 'is exactly the same' and described her global stardom as 'really funny.'

The multiplatinum superstar – who won the Academy Award for Best Original Song in 2022 for the James Bond track No Time to Die – said that she doesn't 'have any friends' outside of the people she works with, who she calls her 'best friends.'

Describing the 'nightmare' of getting recognized at sixteen, Billie said: 'I was just turned into this prop. I've never been so scared. It was like, a huge stampede...'

'From that day on,' she said, 'I didn't go anywhere. I didn't do anything. I was so flipped out by what had happened and how powerless I felt.'

But the last few years have somewhat brightened Billie's perspective when it comes to fame, as she now describes it as an 'unbelievable 50/50.' 

She explained: 'There are so many things that I would never ever be able to do if I didn't have what I have, and so many things that I can never do again.' 

'I live a very high-highs and low-lows type of life,' said Eilish. 'I'm just that kind of person. Everything feels huge or tiny.'
