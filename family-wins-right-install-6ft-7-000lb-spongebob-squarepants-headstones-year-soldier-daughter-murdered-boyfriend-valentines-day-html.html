Family wins right to re-install 6ft SpongeBob SquarePants headstones in Cincinnati cemetery

Publish date: 2024-09-11

Family wins right to re-install 6ft 7,000lb SpongeBob SquarePants headstones a year after their soldier daughter was 'murdered by her boyfriend' on Valentine's Day

  • Sgt. Kimberly Walker, 28, was found strangled to death in a Colorado hotel room in February 2013
  • As a tribute to their loved one, her family decided to build two monuments of her favorite cartoon character at her graveside
  • The Spring Grove Cemetery in Cincinnati gave approval for the statues back in March and they were finally erected on October
  • The next day bosses contacted the family to say that design was 'inappropriate' for such a historic cemetery
  • Spring Grove offered to pay for something more in keeping with the surroundings, but the family want to keep SpongeBob
  • A compromise has more been reached and the original monuments reinstalled with added granite slabs to shield them from passersby

A SpongeBob SquarePants monument commemorating the life of a soldier murdered on Valentine's Day 2013 was reinstalled at a Cincinnati cemetery on Friday, on the first anniversary of the woman's tragic death.

Sgt. Kimberly Walker, 28, was found dead in a Colorado Springs, Colo., hotel room on Feb 14 last year and her boyfriend, also a soldier, was arrested and charged with first-degree murder.

To stand as a tribute to their lost loved one, the Walker family decided it would be fitting to erect two 7,000-pound SpongeBob SquarePants monuments at Walker’s graveside in the historic Spring Grove Cemetery.

Two SpongeBob SquarePants tombstones commemorating the life of Sgt Kimberly Walker, who was murdered on Valentine's Day 2013, have been reinstalled at a Cincinnati cemetery four months after they were taken down

The $26,000 monument, consisting of the two SpongeBob tombstones, was erected on October 10, 2013 at Spring Grove Cemetery's Section 144 only to be dramatically taken down the next day in a dispute over its design.

According to Deborah Walker, Kimberly’s mother, her daughter had loved everything to do with the cartoon character and had countless SpongeBob-branded items including shower curtains.

‘SpongeBob went in her casket before we laid her in the ground,’ she told WLWT.com. in October.

The Walkers had only gone ahead with the unusual and expensive monuments after they had received approval from a cemetery employee last March.

Kimberly Walker, right, was found strangled earlier this year, she was a massive fan of SpongeBob SquarePants and so her family decided that two 6ft monuments of the kid's favorite would be a fitting tribute

‘They stand six feet tall and he’s four feet wide and he’s on a platform of eight inches, so it makes him 6 feet 8 inches tall,’ explained Ms Walker at the time.

Each SpongeBob was created wearing a military uniform, one in an Army uniform for Kimberly and the other in a Navy uniform for twin sister Kara, who is an IT Specialist in the U.S. Navy.

The monuments also had an American flag on one sleeve and the No. 24 for Kimberly’s favorite Nascar driver Jeff Gordon on the other.

‘I thought it was the greatest thing in the cemetery. I even told the people there that I think this is the best monument I’ve ever seen. It’s the best headstone in the cemetery and they all agreed. It came out really nice,’ said sister Kara.

The monuments were finally installed at the cemetery on October 10, but the next day cemetery officials informed the Walker family that the twin SpongeBobs were inappropriate and had to be removed.

Mom Deborah Walker has said that the family want to honor their dead daughter with the SpongeBob statues, while Kimberly's twin sister Kara has described them as 'the greatest thing in the cemetery'

Kimberly's boyfriend Sgt. Montrell Mayo was charged with first-degree murder following her death last February

Spring Grove was quick to accept responsibility for the misunderstanding and promised to work with the family to design at alternative which they hoped would be more in keeping with the historic cemetery.

Now four months later, the monument was back up, and they have issued an apology to the Walker family.

‘Spring Grove Cemetery apologizes to the Walker family. Our personnel oversaw the design and installed the SpongeBob monuments and then had them removed when concerns were raised by others.

We deeply regret the distress this caused the family. We have worked hard to remedy the problem and appreciate the cooperation we received from the family during this process,’ read the statement.

The original monuments were reinstalled Friday but full granite slabs were also erected to shield the headstones from passersby, reports WCPO.

According to Al Gerhardstein, the Walker family’s attorney, the apology has been accepted.

Gerhardstein said the Walkers are pleased the original SpongeBob figures remain included in the monuments.

'The Walker family has other members buried at Spring Grove and it was important to resolve this dispute in a way that would preserve Spring Grove as their family cemetery,' Gerhardstein said.

'The peace the family can now enjoy at Kimberly’s graveside on the beautiful grounds of the historic Spring Grove Cemetery will help them find the strength to endure the upcoming trial of the man accused in her death.'

Kimberley's twin sister Kara, who is an IT Specialist in the U.S. Navy, poses beside the SpongeBob monument that celebrates her sister and is dressed in army fatigues

Kimberly Walker was a massive fan of SpongeBob SquarePants, left, and Nascar driver Jeff Gordon

Following Walker’s murder in February 2013, her boyfriend Sgt. Montrell Mayo was charged with first-degree murder.

The arrest affidavit states that Mayo, a Fort Carson soldier, admitted to his supervisor in a phone call ‘I think I killed my girlfriend’ after the two had an altercation at a hotel in Colorado Springs

The affidavit states that Mayo told his supervisor that Walker threatened his career, then he hit her with a glass and doesn’t remember anything after that

Police who responded to the scene found Walker’s body and blood on the bed sheets, clothing and sink. An autopsy report states that she had been strangled.
