Diane von Furstenberg has offered a rare glimpse inside her Connecticut country estate, which features sprawling gardens, a swimming pool and a giant lipstick sculpture made from a tree trunk.

The 66-year-old fashion designer opened up her home, which she has named Cloudwalk, to Natalie Joos, writer of the blog Tales of Endearment, who roamed the property with a team of models.

The group was also permitted to rifle through Ms von Furstenberg's personal archives of vintage clothes and accessories, which the models tried on for size in a video directed by Ms Joos.

In the video, Ms von Furstenberg wears a black-and-white striped blouse and minimal make-up as she opens up about the fortune and success that she fell into at a young age.

'I started in fashion really by accident,' she explains, adding: 'I didn't know what I wanted to do, but I knew the kind of woman that I wanted to be.'

The glamorous designer originally rose to prominence when she married German Prince Egon of Fürstenberg in 1969.

But she told the New York Times in 1977: 'The minute I knew I was about to be Egon's wife, I decided to have a career. I wanted to be someone of my own, and not just a plain little girl who got married'.

Wardrobe: A video shows models Elizabeth Gilpin, Laura Love and Tali Lennox - daughter of Annie Lennox - trying on looks from the designer's personal archives as they roam the grounds

Trademark: The girls model three of Ms Furstenberg's signature wrap dresses as they march through the grounds under umbrellas

At 26, she invented the wrap dress. 'It was a nothing little printed dress made out of jersey,' she tells Ms Joos in the video.

'Before I knew, I lived an American dream, making more than 25,000 dresses a week.'

Her royal marriage ended in 1972, but her success as a designer carried on. 'I was in the right place, at the right time, and it was something that had not been done,' she said of the wrap dress.

'But I think the thing that made the dress special and timeless is that it was all about the woman. It really flattered a woman's body and it gave her independence and power.'

'I love colors. Imagine a garden in black-and-white, how sad it would be'

In another shot, two of the models, both wearing bold, monochrome polka-dotted ensembles, recline on an equally eye-catching tiger print sofa.

'I like everything that is effortless,' she says of her fashion vision, before adding: 'Well there's humor. There's the yin and the yang, you know, I like the little twists.'

Inside Ms von Furstenberg's closet - which, Ms Joos notes, not even her granddaughter Talita knew about before the video - the group discover vintage Louboutin heels, fringe pants, fur boots, Stetson hats and, of course, wrap dresses in every color and pattern imaginable.

'I love colors,' says the designer. 'You know, if people ask me about colors, I just say, "Imagine a garden in black and white, how sad it would be."'

Indeed, she explains that sometimes fashion is taken too seriously, something she expressly does not do.

'I always made everybody smile,' she says. 'And people don't understand. They say, "Really?" And I say, "Yes. Joie de vivre."

Despite her long career and immense success, Ms von Furstenberg says she believes her greatest fashion moment is yet to come.

'It's just that all of a sudden, all the pieces – the old pieces, the new pieces – they're all kind of gelling,' she tells Ms Joos.

Group shot: At the end of the video, Ms von Furstenberg looks just as glamorous as ever as she strikes a pose next to Ms Joos and the three young models

Bright side: 'I always made everybody smile,' Ms von Furstenberg says of her designs. 'And people don't understand. They say, "Really?" And I say, "Yes. Joie de vivre"'

Creativity: 'Women inspire me, images inspire me, details inspire me. Old photographs,' she says

'And it's like. . .  for the first time, as crazy as it may seem, I'm kind of embracing the whole thing. So I think that in a weird way. . .' she trails off, before saying: 'I don't know. I always like the present.'

Ms Joos writes in her piece that she and the models were awe-struck by the incredible items in Ms Furstenberg's, some of which date back to the Sixties and Seventies.

'I don't have to wear vintage. I am vintage'

But the designer asserts that when she started out, 'I didn't even know about the word vintage or anything like that.

'Women inspire me, images inspire me, details inspire me. Old photographs,' she says.

At the end of the video, Ms von Furstenberg looks just as glamorous as ever as she strikes a pose next to Ms Joos and the three young models.

'I don't have to wear vintage,' she says to the camera. 'I am vintage.'
