William Shatner reveals Star Trek's Leonard Nimoy helped him with death ofwife

Publish date: 2024-10-02

William Shatner has revealed his Star Trek co-star Leonard Nimoy helped him cope after his alcoholic wife drowned in a swimming pool.

The 84-year-old had only been married to former model Nerine Kidd for two years when he found her naked and unconscious in the grounds of their Los Angeles home in 1999.

A post-mortem later revealed she a blood-alcohol level of 0.28 percent when she entered the pool at the Studio City mansion, three times the legal alcohol limit for drivers. Traces of the sedative drug Valium were also found in her system

First mate: William Shatner has revealed his Star Trek sidekick Leonard Nimoy helped him cope with his alcoholic wife Nerine's death drowning death in 1999

During an interview which aired on SiriusXM's Tell Me Everything Monday, he said: said: 'Leonard helped me understand, try to understand.

'Yes, agony belongs to all of us. Agony is party of the soul. Somebody who hasn't felt agony hasn't lived, hasn't participated in what life is.

'But there is a special agony for somebody who wants to give up the addiction and can't. The shame that they must feel. The self-degradation. So that's another agony that people who don't (suffer from addiction)... don't feel that shame that makes you want to die.

When my wife died I felt such agony. My illumination was, "my God, this is what she felt.' I couldn't stand the pain. I felt a pain so intense... that I wanted to die. 

Third wife: He had only been married to the model for two years when he found her dead in his swimming pool

'I thought, "OK I get it, that's why people commit suicide." Because the pain overwhelms the desire to live. And most people don't feel that, don't reach bottom that way.' 

Shatner, who is known worldwide for his turn as Captain James T Kirk, also said: 'I don't understand the agony. I am empathetic to the agony. I am in agony myself, not about drinking, not about addiction, but whatever my agonies are.'

Nimoy became an alcoholic in the sixties while starring as Vulcan Mr Spock on the original TV incarnation Star Trek, and he later sought treatment in rehab.

Speaking about his troubles, he said: 'I started drinking regularly, ritually, during the second or third year of our series.

Old friend: Shatner said Nimoy was able to help him understand as he had also been an alcoholic

'The minute we finished the last shot I would have a drink. Then it became a series of drinks, little by little.

'Before I knew it I was drinking more and more because my addictive personality was taking over.' 

Shatner spoke out as he promoted his new book on his co-star, who died last year at the age of 83 due to cancer, Leonard: My Fifty-Year Friendship with a Remarkable Man 

The Canadian actor famously missed the Invasion Of The Body Snatchers star's funeral following as it clashed with a charity appearance. 

Driven to drink: Nimoy revealed he developed an addiction after shooting to fame alongside Shatner on Trek

Nerine, who was the TJ Hooker star's third wife, broke her neck when she dived or fell into the water. 

The pair considered divorce in 1998, but later reconciled. Her cause of death was listed as drowning associated with neck trauma. It was ruled an accident.

At the time of the accident, he said: 'She meant everything to me. Her laughter, her tears and her joy will remain with me the rest of my life.'

He has been married to his current wife Elizabeth since 2001.
