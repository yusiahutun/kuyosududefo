Best thriller books | Page-turners you can't put down

1

Colin Walsh, 'Kala'

1

Colin Walsh, 'Kala'

Now 15% Off

If you're a fan of Tana French or Donna Tartt, you'll love Colin Walsh – this year, he was shortlisted for the Waterstones Debut Fiction prize for his bestselling novel, Kala. It follows the story of three old friends, thrown together for the first time in years in the seaside town of Kinlough, on Ireland's west coast. As teenagers, they were friends with the reckless and brilliant Kala Lanann – who then disappeared without a trace. But when human remains are discovered and past and present begin to collide, the estranged friends are forced to confront their own complicity in what happened.

2

Jessica Knoll, 'Bright Young Women'

2

Jessica Knoll, 'Bright Young Women'

From the author of the New York Times bestseller – now a hit Netflix film – Luckiest Girl Alive comes Jessica Knoll's extraordinary new novel, based on the real-life sorority targeted by America's first celebrity serial killer, Ted Bundy. Knoll takes on Bundy's story from the perspective of a student who survives one of his horrific attacks, and writes with a searing, vivid attention to detail which is all-consuming. The title nods to the author's fiercely feminist stance, as she follows the stories of the ‘bright young women’ affected by Bundy – both the victims snuffed out in their glorious prime, and those left behind in their wake.

3

Nita Prose, 'The Mystery Guest'

3

Nita Prose, 'The Mystery Guest'

Now 53% Off

If you have yet to read The Maid by Nita Prose, make sure you order and devour it now before the sequel is released. A bestseller that focuses on heroine Molly Gray – a hotel maid and amateur sleuth – The Maid is followed up by The Mystery Guest, which sees Molly try to solve the murder of a famous mystery writer at the Regency Grand Hotel. Fun, tightly written and reminiscent of Agatha Christie's whodunnits, this is a charming thriller with heart and wit.

4

Stephen King, 'Holly'

4

Stephen King, 'Holly'

Now 48% Off

Does anyone write thrillers quite as deftly as Stephen King? The prolific master of the field has just published his 65th novel, Holly, which reintroduces Holly Gibney, one of King's most compelling, beloved and resourceful characters. In this eponymous novel, she returns to solve the gruesome truth behind multiple disappearances in a Midwestern town – but she'll come up against some unimaginably depraved and brilliantly disguised adversaries in the process. Like all of King's novels, this is a pleasure to read, faultlessly constructed and slickly executed.

5

Rachel Hawkins, 'The Villa'

5

Rachel Hawkins, 'The Villa'

If you like Taylor Jenkins Reid, you'll love this. A dark, twisty tale that's a New York Times bestseller for good reason, The Villa tells the story of two childhood best friends who reconnect as adults at an Italian villa with a dark past. As secrets from the villa's history come to light, equally dangerous betrayals from the present day emerge – and it looks as if horrifying events are set to occur once again.The perfect holiday read (though maybe not if you're renting a villa...).

6

Nora Murphy, 'The New Mother'

6

Nora Murphy, 'The New Mother'

Now 47% Off

Nora Murphy has form when it comes to writing can’t-put-it-down thrillers (her previous novel, The Favour, was a bestseller for a reason). In The New Mother, she returns to her favourite haunt of eerily lonely suburbia, this time putting a bone-chilling twist on isolating new motherhood. When Natalie gives birth to her son, her husband works long hours, leaving her to navigate a new house, child and neighbourhood alone. Paul, Natalie's next door neighbour, befriends her, which feels like a lifeline – until it becomes clear that Paul wants something in return…

7

Ashley Audrain, 'The Whispers'

7

Ashley Audrain, 'The Whispers'

In her 2021 debut The Push, Ashley Audrain showed off her talent for exposing the raw, ugly side of motherhood. This latest novel, The Whispers, expands upon this theme, centring on a shocking event that shatters the lives of a family and those around them. Jacob and Whitney Loverly are the couple everyone wants to be, but when their son is hospitalised after a mysterious accident, everyone on the family's tight-knit street must reckon with some hard truths. With a breathless pace and poignant observations about the realities of motherhood and womanhood today, this is a smart and eminently readable thriller.

8

Ling Ling Huang, 'Natural Beauty'

8

Ling Ling Huang, 'Natural Beauty'

A razor-sharp read for fans of an unconventional thriller, Natural Beauty follows a young musician who enters an elite world obsessed with appearance – with dark consequences. The narrator, a talented piano player, abandons her job in music when an accident leaves her parents debilitated, instead going to work at Holistik, a high-end wellness store in New York. Her new job affords her entry into a world of privilege and gives her a long-awaited sense of belonging, as she is plied with products that slim her thighs, smooth her skin and lighten her hair. But beneath these creams and tinctures lies something sinister. A piercing and witty debut, Natural Beauty explores questions of consumerism, self-worth, race, and identity – and will leave you with a shocking and unsettling truth.

9

Lexie Elliott, 'Bright and Deadly Things'

9

Lexie Elliott, 'Bright and Deadly Things'

Following the death of her husband, Emily is happy to find herself surrounded by her friends and fellow Oxford peers at the rustic Chalet des Anglais in the French Alps. With no electricity, running water or access by car, she hopes the trip will offer her the time and space she needs to heal. But a break-in just before she leaves for the airport, coupled with a tryst she unwittingly sees between an undergraduate and a colleague, sets the scene for a retreat that's all but relaxing. The ideal locked-room drama, this will have you hooked from the very first page.

10

Lindsay Cameron, 'No One Needs to Know'

10

Lindsay Cameron, 'No One Needs to Know'

This wonderfully juicy tale of Upper East Side mothers gone bad is Big Little Lies meets Gossip Girl, with extra drama thrown in for good measure. When UrbanMyth, an anonymous neighbourhood forum containing the darkest secrets of Manhattan's wealthiest elite gets hacked, everything is laid bare: secret bank accounts, affairs and worse. When a 'hacktivist' reveals the identity of each poster, the repercussions are huge – and deadly. Cameron's pacey, character-driven style makes this deep-dive into the super-rich an essential for your summer reading list.

11

Wendy Heard, 'You Can Trust Me'

11

Wendy Heard, 'You Can Trust Me'

If you loved the adventures of Mia and Lucia in The White Lotus, then you'll adore Wendy Heard's You Can Trust Me, a twisted tale of two scamming women who push their luck just that little bit too far. Heard explores the underworld of the one-percent through the eyes of Summer, a perpetual drifter, and her partner in crime, Leo. When Leo thinks she might have snagged the ultimate catch in the form of a tech billionaire, it seems the women have got lucky – but then Leo mysteriously vanishes, and Summer has to rely on her years of experience to find her. The fact that most of the action takes place on a private island where nothing is as it appears only adds to this delicious mystery, making it the ideal beach read.

12

Disha Bose, 'Dirty Laundry'

12

Disha Bose, 'Dirty Laundry'

A book that embodies the (slightly updated) adage to 'keep your friends close and your neighbours closer', Dirty Laundry is an intelligent and marvellously well-observed commentary on the gossipy landscape of suburbia. Ciara has it all – a loving husband, well-behaved children and an immaculate home – but behind the filters, her reality is far from what it seems. Mishti is stuck in a loveless marriage, raising her daughter in a country that is too cold, among children who look nothing like her. Lauren is mostly happy, despite being judged for letting her kids run naked, wild and free. Then Ciara is found murdered in her pristine home and suddenly everyone is a suspect... A great new suburban noir (think Paula Hawkins' The Girl On The Train).

13

Andrea Bartz, 'The Spare Room'

13

Andrea Bartz, 'The Spare Room'

When a book garners praise from the likes of Lucy Foley and Nita Prose, masters of the genre, you know it's going to be an edge-of-your-seat thriller. The Spare Room doesn't disappoint: when Kelly finds herself adrift during lockdown, she sends an Instagram DM to Sabrina, a former high-school classmate who is now a bestselling novelist with an enviable mansion and husband. When Kelly's fiancé tells her he may not want to get married after all, she makes the decision to visit Sabrina, and initially enjoys their risky world and open marriage. But then, Kelly discovers that the last woman they invited in is missing – and she starts to wonder what might happen to her, too... Dark and gripping with entertaining bite, this is a novel that's almost impossible not to enjoy.

14

Jesse Sutanto, 'Vera Wong's Unsolicited Advice for Murderers'

14

Jesse Sutanto, 'Vera Wong's Unsolicited Advice for Murderers'

A thriller in name but very different to any you'll have read before, this superb mystery from Jesse Sutanto is witty, charming and fiendish. Featuring 60-something entrepreneur-amateur sleuth Vera Wong, who springs into action when she stumbles on a body in the middle of her tea shop, this is a cosy drama written with style and panache. Fans of Richard Osman's work will adore this.

15

Megan Abbott, 'Beware the Woman'

15

Megan Abbott, 'Beware the Woman'

Dark and disturbing, this psychological thriller places a young woman at the mercy of her potentially sinister partner's family. Newly married and with a baby on the way, Jacy and her new husband Jed embark on a road trip to visit Jed's father, a doctor living in Michigan. Everything seems perfect, until Jacy has a health scare – and begins to feel trapped in the house. Are her fears paranoia, cabin fever, or could something awful be about to happen? Beware the Woman is a claustrophobic masterclass in tense suspicion.
