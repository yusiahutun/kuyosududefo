Julia Garner exudes Old Hollywood glamour in all green at the premiere of Asteroid City in Cannes

Publish date: 0001-01-01

Julia Garner stunned in all green as she debuted a new platinum up-do with straight hair at the premiere of Asteroid City at the 76th annual Cannes Film Festival on Tuesday.

The Inventing Anna actress, 29, exuded old Hollywood glamour with her look, donning a green Gucci strapless dress with a flowing train.

Julia was all smiles on the red carpet as she showed off the garment which also featured a cut out detail along the chest.

As the beauty held out her arms, the true scale of the ensemble was revealed with wispy fabric falling down in a waterfall-style effect.  

Wearing her platinum locks in an up-do, she made a departure from her signature curly locks for a sleek and chic look.  

Green envy: Julia Garner, 29, exuded Old Hollywood glamour as she debuted a straight platinum up-do at the premiere of Asteroid City on Tuesday at the Cannes Film Festival

Stunning: The richly-coloured gown boasted a gorgeous train that trailed behind her 

The Ozark star drew further attention to her look with a pair of towering heels to accentuate her slim frame. 

The futuristic caper Asteroid City is playing in competition for the coveted Palm d'Or at this year's ceremony, and once again an array of A-list talent was display to ensure it was given a grand reception on the resort town's iconic Croisette. 

Leading the way ahead of Tuesday's screening, Rosie Huntington-Whiteley commanded attention in a sweeping white gown with a thigh-high split as she crossed the red carpet, where she had good company in the form of actresses Brie Larson and Julia Garner. 

The look comes just a day after Julia looked effortlessly stylish in a smart white co-ord as she attended Naomi Campbell's star studded birthday party on Monday evening in Cannes. 

The actress flashed her toned midriff in a chic plunging waistcoat which boated large silver dazzling buttons. 

She added a pair of coordinated wide legged trousers to the ensemble as she posed on the purple carpet at the Boss X Naomi event hosted by Daniel Grieder during Cannes Film Festival. 

The Ozark star added inches to her frame in a glamorous pair of crisp white pointed toe heels and toted her belongings around in a black square clutch. 

Julia accessorised with one eye catching dazzling earring and styled her short blonde locks in luxurious curls.

She is best known for her starring role as Ruth Langmore in the ongoing Netflix crime drama series Ozark, which first hit screens in 2017.

This portrayal earned her two Primetime Emmy Awards for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series in 2019 and 2020.

She is also an established big screen actress, having starred in titles including The Perks Of Being A Wallflower and We Are What We Are.

Julia has sparked feverish excitement with her turn in a Madonna biopic - although confusion spread with claims that the project has been scrapped.

Earlier this month, Madonna renewed hope the 'scrapped,' self-directed Universal Pictures biopic - titled Little Sparrow - might get resurrected after she and Julia hung out in Manhattan's Flatiron district.

The 64-year-old Queen of Pop - who boasts 51.3M social media followers - took to Instagram to share a snap of herself munching on French fries with the actress, declaring: 'B**** we're Madonna!'

After the Met Gala, Michigan-born Madge and Bronx-born Julia attended the 58th birthday of famed lensman Steven Klein at immersive fine-dining restaurant Journey featuring 'floor-to-ceiling and tabletop 360 projection mapping' - according to the New York Post.

It's been nearly a year since Garner was first attached to star in the biopic, but she told ET on March 12 she was still optimistic: 'I don't want to tell too much. I want to keep things... yeah, fingers crossed!'

On August 26, the gossip site DeuxMoi shared an anonymous tip indicating that Sparrow was a 'nightmare production' because of the 'script and constant changes' and 'might not happen' because it's 'costing a ton of money.'
