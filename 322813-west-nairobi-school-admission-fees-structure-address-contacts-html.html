West Nairobi School admission, fees structure, address, contacts

Publish date: 2024-02-17

Giving your child a good education is a huge investment in their future. Today, quality education means taking your child to a good school, where he/she can access the best learning resources. However, with the proliferation of substandard schools, this is easier said than done. Luckily, there are still some good schools in the country. One such school is the West Nairobi School (WNS).

West Nairobi School is one of the leading international schools in Nairobi. It is a Christian co-ed international institution. It bases its curriculum on the North American school system. If you need to enrol your kid to the school, here's more information about the school, from the curriculum and academics offered to application admission, fees, and more.

An overview of West Nairobi School

West Nairobi School was started in 1996 in Nairobi. It was started to offer quality North American Christian education for members of the Summer Institute of Linguistics while on missions in Kenya. Quality affordable international education in Kenya was hard to find at the time.

When starting up, the institution only had 46 students and offered curriculum from Kindergarten to 8th Grade only. Over the years, the school has grown, becoming one of the top international learning institutes in the country.

It attracts students from all over the world and offers learning opportunities for students from elementary school (early childhood and kindergarten) to secondary school (Grade 12). The school is accredited by both the Association of Christian Schools International (ACSI) and the Middle States Association Commission on Elementary and Secondary Schools (MSA-CES).

West Nairobi School academics & curriculum

West Nairobi School follows the North American school system, the same one used in the United States. Under this system, students learn from Kindergarten to Grade 12 (commonly referred to as K-12).

1. Elementary school

The institution offers a complete kindergarten curriculum that nurtures young minds and prepares them for further learning. It also has an early childhood centre for young kids of about 3 to four years. The centre prepares them for introduction into the K-12 system. The elementary school also comprises Grade 1 to 5, or what we commonly refer to as the primary school under the UK school system.

2. Secondary school

After Grade 5, students proceed on to secondary school. This level is divided into two: middle school (from Grade 6 to Grade 8) and high school (from Grade 9 to Grade 12). Usually, students graduate from Grade 12 at about 17 or 18 years.

3. Advanced placement program

Advanced Placement is a program offered under the North American curriculum, where students can take college-level courses and exams in high school. As a result, they get several benefits when pursuing a college education. These benefits include college credits, skipped introductory college classes, and a better profile for college admissions.

4. Languages & Learning support

West Nairobi School offers an English-based curriculum. However, being an international school, there are several students whose first language is not English. To help them adjust to the school and keep up with other students, the institution offers an English Language Learner program. The program is designed to improve the students' English reading, writing, and comprehension skills.

The school also has a learning support program for students with minor learning disabilities, or who are behind academically. Both programs have teachers who work one-on-one with students to improve their learning abilities.

5. Christian-based education

West Nairobi School is a Christian school and thus incorporates religious values with the learning processes. There is a chapel in the institution, where a service is held once every week. They require students to attend the service, where they are joined by teachers and other staff.

6. Technology integrated learning

The institution aims to offer the best education to the students, which is why it incorporates technology into the learning experience. There are computer labs that are fully equipped with a range of learning resources. Elementary school students also enjoy Kindles and Chrome books, which offer digital learning resources.

What extracurricular activities are offered at West Nairobi School?

West Nairobi School is not all about academics, it also has a very vibrant extra-circular program that engages the students outside of the classroom. The activities offered under the program include:

1. Sports

Sports is one of the top extra-curricular activities at the school. Students are urged to take part in competitive sports to grow physically, mentally, emotionally, and spiritually. Sports programs available at the school include basketball, football (soccer), volleyball, cross-country, and swimming.

3. Clubs

The school offers several clubs, including drama, choir, band, strings, ballet, STEM (science, technology, engineering, and mathematics), Taekwondo, ballet, dance, crotchet, and gardening. Extra-curricular activities such as art, volleyball, and swimming also have their own clubs.

Admission process for West Nairobi School

West Nairobi School is open to students from both Kenya and other international countries. However, students seeking to enroll in the school must have a working knowledge of the English language. Those who are not proficient can benefit from the ELL program.

Admission is open across all levels, from early childhood programs to kindergarten, elementary school, and secondary school. However, each student must take a placement test to determine. Those who are transferring from another school offering the North American curriculum can, however, be exempted from the tests.

How much does it cost to learn at West Nairobi School?

The West Nairobi School fees structure varies depending on the level of education and is subject to change according to the schools' guidelines. The fee structure is inclusive of the tuition fees, extra-curricular activities, transportation (optional), and lunch (catered lunch is offered). The admission fee is $185 while the exact figures for tuition will be communicated to the parent upon showing your interest in enrolling your child.

Where is West Nairobi School located?

WNS first opened its first campus at Argwings Kodhek Road in the Kilimani area. This served as the base of the school until 2000, when it moved to its current campus in Karen, Nairobi.
