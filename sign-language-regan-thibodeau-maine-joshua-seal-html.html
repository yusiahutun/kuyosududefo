EXCLUSIVE: Sign language interpreter who was front and center of Maine mass shooter media briefings

Publish date: 2024-05-13

The signer at the media briefings about Maine mass shooter Robert Card lost one of her close friends who was also deaf to the killer, DailyMail.com can exclusively reveal.

Regan Thibodeau, 44, stands next to officials and dramatically relays their words in American sign language for the benefit of hearing impaired people watching around the country.

She worked with murdered Joshua Seal, 36, who was one of the four deaf people slaughtered as they were playing cornhole when Card, 40, stormed into Schemengees Bar & Grill firing an AR-15-style assault rifle.

And in a further tragic twist, Regan revealed loving father Joshua would have been working with her at the media briefings if he hadn't been one of a total of eight people killed at the bar on Wednesday night.

Regan Thibodeau, 44, is seen signing on Friday night during the press conference announcing Robert Card's death

Thibodeau is seen on Friday evening signing for Governor Janet Mills

Joshua Seal, a friend and colleague of Tibodeau's, was killed in Wednesday's shooting

Regan bravely told DailyMail.com after signing Friday tonight at Lewiston's city hall as officials announced Card's body had been found: 'I was born and raised here in Maine. My colleague was actually murdered, Joshua Seal.

'He actually would have been the person here working with me. And because he's gone I'm here working alone. So he was one of the people that we lost.'

Regan said she did not want to dwell on emotion, but emphasize the importance of communicating to everybody despite the obvious pain over the tragedy.

'I have been very focused on the work that has been providing the access to service which is primary to me, because all deaf people, they are my community, they are my home,' she said. 

'And they should have access and the right to know what's going on.

'It's so crucial to have this access to communication. So this service is really in honor and memoriam to Joshua. And all the deaf people we lost.

'I'm not taking time to process this personally myself. I am just focusing on providing the communication access.'

Robert Card, 40, has been found dead, multiple news outlets report. Card is accused of killing 18 people in a series of shootings in Lewiston, Maine on Wednesday night

Regan, wearing all black, has been a feature of the briefings since they began the day after Card's horrific slaughter at the bar and Sparetime Recreation bowling alley. She will be on hand on Saturday as officials are expected to give a further briefing on Card's final moments.

She said: 'In this situation specifically it directly impacted the deaf community because there were members of that community who were murdered, four of them that were killed by the hand of this gunman.

'So that means we have hundreds of hundreds of thousands of people watching this news.

'Not only was it important that those people whose primary language is American sign language to get that information but it was also important to see a hearing and deaf interpreter working together so that we can meet the wider range of the deaf community's language needs â€“ and providing the model for communication access for the media.

'Any time there is an issue that affects the general public such as health, wellbeing, public safety, it's important to include everybody that is involved so they can all have access to the communication that contributes to the community.'

Card was found dead in the woods on Friday with a self-inflicted gunshot wound to his head - ending a 48-hour manhunt involving over 300 members of law enforcement.

Androscoggin sheriff's office wrote on Facebook: 'The suspect in Wednesday nights shootings has been located and is deceased.'

Card, 40, was found at 7:45pm near Lisbon Falls, where his car was abandoned shortly after Wednesday's massacre. His body was reportedly located near a recycling plant from where he was recently fired.

It was unclear how long he had been dead, and Mike Sauschuck, the commander of Maine's department of public safety, would not say whether he had died as police closed in or been dead for days. 

'There continues to be a lot of work that needs to be done here at the scene, and a lot to follow up on,' said Sauschuck.  

Janet Mills, the governor of Maine, said that she was 'breathing a sigh of relief knowing Card is no longer a threat to anyone'

The 40-year-old is seen during his rampage through Lewiston, murdering 18 people and injuring more than a dozen others on Wednesday night

Card, a US Army reserve soldier, launched the initial attack at a bowling alley (pictured) at around 6.56pm then struck again at bar and restaurant four miles away just 12 minutes later

Sauschuck confirmed that he is believed to have died from a self-inflicted gunshot wound. 

He added that a hunting ban in the area has now been lifted. Saturday marks the start of the deer hunting season, and is a key date in Maine's calendar.
