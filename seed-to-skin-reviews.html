#TheBrand | Seed To Skin

Publish date: 2024-10-05

In our regular feature #TheBrand, Bazaar’s beauty team look into an exciting and efficacious brand taking the beauty industry by storm. This time, it’s a skincare line combining plant extracts with advanced science.

For Jeanette Thottrup, owner of Tuscany’s Borgo Santo Pietro hotel, a sprawling 270-acre estate makes for rich pickings when it comes to not only food, but potent skincare ingredients too.

What’s the story?

Jeanette and husband Claus Thottrup moved into the Borgo Santo Pietro resort in 2001, undertaking a large renovation project to transform the estate into a boutique hotel, complete with all the typically Tuscan trappings - think abundant organic farming, a cookery school and Michelin-starred restaurant (two, in fact). With a background in natural medicine – and time spent working with Neal's Yard Remedies – Thottrup decided to eschew a third-party brand for the hotel's spa, instead creating her own line.

What’s in it?

Nestled between the cheesemaking dairy and tennis courts is an advanced skincare lab, where each product for Seed to Skin – the hotel’s own beauty project – is created. Chiming with the industry’s growing focus on transparency, the brand happily places its ingredient lists in the spotlight.

By keeping the entire production process in house, Thottrup maintains a firm grasp on every step, meaning total traceability when it comes to raw materials, extraction methods and formulation – as well as sustainable practices. Everything is either sourced from a list of trusted growers, or produced on the estate.

But while the purist appeal of natural ingredients is clearly present in each of Seed to Skin’s products, there’s also serious results to be found in these weighty glass jars. Equal focus is placed on effectivity, with many of the formulas taking over four years to bring to fruition. (After all, the benefits of an on-site lab include limitless testing, tweaking and experimenting.)

Applying molecular science to natural ingredients, every product is designed to work on multiple layers of the skin, with key ingredients penetrating the epidermis to deliver long-term results that are more than just skin deep.

“I have always loved the integrity of natural skincare, and have studied the healing powers of plants – but with Seed To Skin, I wanted to see how far I could push the efficacy of plant extracts with the help of science.” says Thottrup. “Plants themselves can deliver potent benefits to the skin, but using the correct extraction methods and formulations is vital.”

What skin types will it suit?

Like many emerging brands, Seed To Skin's focus is on glowing skin, rather than militant 'anti' ageing or problem correcting. So, if you're looking to enhance radiance, this one's for you.

Very sensitive skins, however, should scour the ingredients lists beforehand (visible at Net-A-Porter), as many contain potentially irritating essential oils such as lavender and peppermint.

What should I try?

A best-seller, The Divine Cleanse is a good marker of what to expect from the brand: it’s rich, decadent, but most importantly, incredibly effective. Formulated with green clay and boosted by marine plankton, this sensorial balm goes the extra mile when it comes to removing impurities and excess oil.

Another stand-out, The Midnight Miracle face oil deeply hydrates while encouraging cell turnover, leading to brighter, fresher skin in time. Multiple rose extracts are combined with hyaluronic acid and vitamins A and E to create a zero-filler blend that sinks seamlessly into a dry complexion. Cocktail it with The Cure night cream, or apply neat after cleansing.

Surprisingly, the most indulgent product of them all is in fact found in the body-care arena. Everything about The Awakening salt scrub is decadent, from the dangerously weighty packaging (this is not one to stuff on a fragile shelf), to the circulation-boosting formula, rich in algae and spirulina. It’s definitely not essential, but it’s exactly the kind of winter indulgence you’ll want to succumb to after the busy festive period.
