Number one singles every year from 2010 to 2019 in decade's music review

Publish date: 2024-03-19

Ten years ago One Direction were still picking zits, there was no Instagram, we downloaded music and Simon Cowell had a completely different face.

Unlike previous decades the Teenies didn’t have a specific sound or haircut to sum it up by.

Instead it was an era where genres and tribes all but disappeared and everyone became partial to a bit of everything, musically speaking.

Here’s a wee trip down memory lane to remind you of the songs and events that became the soundtrack to each of the past 10 years...

2010: Love The Way You Lie – Eminem featuring Rihanna

Incredibly this anthem never ­actually topped the charts, however it did spend a massive 14 consecutive weeks in the Top 10 of the Official Singles Chart.

2011: Someone Like You – Adele

Before every X Factor wannabe on the planet sang it, the north Londoner spent four weeks at No1 with it. To this day has never dropped out of the Top 100.

2012: Somebody That I Used To Know – Gotye featuring Kimbra

The Belgian-born Australian hit the jackpot with this moving anthem, spending five weeks at the top of the charts and 18 in the Top 10. He hasn’t been heard of since!

2013: Blurred Lines – Robin Thicke featuring T.I. and Pharrell Williams

Success aside this song is now best known as one of the most controversial hits of the decade. Pharrell recently confessed feeling “embarrassed” by the unsubtle lyrics, while the Marvin Gaye estate successfully won a payout over claims of plagiarism.

2014: Happy – Pharrell Williams

Still the most downloaded track of all time, this was Pharrell’s third UK No1, and originally written for the soundtrack of kids cartoon, Despicable Me.

2015: Uptown Funk – Mark Ronson and Bruno Mars

This party fave has broken its own streaming record three times in the UK, pushing 2.56million streams in a single week. Uptown Funk is one of only five songs to top the US and UK ­singles chart for at least seven weeks each.

2016: One Dance – Drake featuring Wizkid and Kyla

Spending 15 consecutive weeks at the top of the charts means this is the longest UK running No1 since downloads started counting towards chart positions.

2017: Shape Of You – Ed Sheeran

Originally written for Rihanna, the week this was released Ed ­occupied the top two positions in the UK charts thanks to Castle On The Hill. It spent 14 weeks at No1 and Ed later released a version with Stormzy.

2018: One Kiss – Calvin Harris and Dua Lipa

This 90s house throwback is both Calvin and Dua’s longest-running chart topper at eight weeks. In fact it’s the third longest running No1 of the century by a female.

2019: Someone You Loved – Lewis Capaldi

Seven weeks at No1 and almost a year of sitting inside the Top 40, this tragic ballad propelled the Scotsman to stardom. Even the guys across The Pond can’t stop listening as it topped the US charts.
