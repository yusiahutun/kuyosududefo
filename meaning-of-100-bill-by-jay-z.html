Meaning of 100$ Bill by JAY-Z

Publish date: 2024-02-08

JAY-Z's song "100$ Bill" delves into the complexities of wealth, power, and the pursuit of success. The song, featured in the soundtrack for the film "The Great Gatsby," offers listeners a glimpse into the extravagant and indulgent lifestyle often associated with excess wealth. Through various stand-out lyrics, JAY-Z explores the darker side of money, its influence on society, and the different paths individuals take to achieve financial success.One standout lyric from the song is "Benjamin Franklins filled, fold it just for the thrill." This lyric highlights the allure of money, the excitement that comes with accumulating wealth, and the desire to showcase one's success. It touches on a theme of self-indulgence and the notion that money can provide not only financial security but also a thrilling sense of satisfaction.Another noteworthy lyric is "Stock markets just crash, now I'm just a bill. History don't repeat itself, it rhymes, 1929, still. Write like Mark Twain, Jay Gatsby, I park things." Here, JAY-Z brings attention to the cyclical nature of the economy and the tendency for history to repeat itself. The reference to the stock market crash of 1929 alludes to the unpredictability and fragility of financial markets. JAY-Z's claim to "write like Mark Twain" and reference to Jay Gatsby further highlights the theme of the pursuit of wealth and success.Furthermore, the lyric "Dollars fall on the skin, some might call it sin" explores the moral implications of acquiring wealth. It suggests that the pursuit of money can be seen as exploitation or sinful. This lyric raises questions about the ethical choices individuals make in their pursuit of financial gain. It delves into the idea that the lavish lifestyle portrayed in the song might come at a cost, both for the individual and society as a whole.Additionally, JAY-Z addresses the role of power and politics in the pursuit of wealth with the lyric "Politicians all move for money, what the hell are we calling 'em?" This line highlights the influence that money can have on politics and the manipulation of power for personal gain. It suggests that politicians, who should serve the public, are often driven by financial motivations. This theme connects back to the idea of wealth as a driving force in society and the challenges it poses to genuine progress and equality.Another standout lyric is "New heroines, new Marilyns, move coke through Maryland." This lyric reveals the darker side of wealth and fame, referencing the use of drugs to sustain the lavish lifestyle. It signifies the allure and danger associated with the pursuit of success. JAY-Z touches on the idea that wealth can lead to self-destructive behaviors and a loss of morality.In a broader sense, "100$ Bill" explores the complexities and consequences of the pursuit of wealth and success.
