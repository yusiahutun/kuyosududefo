Career and Professional Life

Moving on to her career, she began working as an Assistant District Attorney at Westchester County Court in 1975. She was also elected Westchester County Executive in 1986.

That same year, she announced her candidacy for lieutenant governor, but she withdrew as her husband could not disclose his business information, which was a requirement.

Similarly, she was elected as Westchester County’s first female judge in November 1990 after running as a Republican against Democratic and Right to Life Party nominees. She held the position until 2005.

She did not seek re-election to a fourth term after being re-elected in 1997 and 2001. Furthermore, she published the non-fiction book titled To Punish and Protect in 2003. She announced in August 2005 that she would run for the U.S.

Senator from New York for the Republican nomination against Senator Hillary Clinton but later she dropped out of the race in December 2005. Later, she began pursuing a career in the media after her political career ended.

She appeared in the shows like ‘The Morning Show with Mike and Juliet,’ a Fox morning talk show, ‘Good Day New York,’ and a Fox 5 morning show. She wrote her novel titled Sly Fox published in 2012.

Additionally, she appeared in HBO’s six-part serial titled The Jinx which aired in 2015. Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, she was also seen hosting the show from home in March 2020.

It was announced on January 12, 2022, that she would be a permanent co-host on the show titled The Five which aired from January 24, 2022.
