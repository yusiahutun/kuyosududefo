The Kansas City Chiefs' Upcoming Super Bowl Trip Is Full of Taylor Swift Easter Eggs

Publish date: 2024-02-03

Of course we Swifties would make the Super Bowl about us.

The Kansas City Chiefs took down the Baltimore Ravens on Sunday night, 17–10—meaning that tight end Travis Kelce and his teammates will head to Super Bowl LVIII in just under two weeks! Taylor Swift ran to the field after yesterday’s game to celebrate the huge milestone with her man, and was pictured getting emotional while congratulating him.

The couple embraced, kissed, laughed, and enjoyed the moment alongside Kelce’s family and teammates. Swift, of course, was in Chiefs red, in the form of a baggy knit sweater from Guest in Residence and her signature bright red lipstick. She also wore a Chiefs necklace.

Patrick Smith//Getty Images

As big a moment as the win was for the Chiefs and the NFL, Swifties on social media quickly found a way to make it all about their Grammy-winning queen by searching for Easter eggs.

For example, fans realized the “Karma” singer has so far attended 12 of Kelce’s games, meaning her appearance at Super Bowl LVIII will mark the 13th—and we all know 13 is Swift’s magic number.

Also, the 2024 Super Bowl falls on February 11, which is 2/11—and 2 + 11 = 13. Also, this year will be the 58th Super Bowl, and five plus eight is what? Thirteen. And that’s not all. The time it will take for Swift to fly from Tokyo (where she’s scheduled to perform the day before the championship game) to Las Vegas (where the game takes place) is roughly … 13 hours. To top it all off, the Chiefs are playing the San Francisco 49ers at the Super Bowl—and 4 + 9 = 13.

It could all be a wild coincidence, but it could also just be that Swift is a mastermind.

Fans have also speculated online that Swift may put on a surprise performance at this year’s Super Bowl halftime show at Allegiant Stadium—especially after all of these 13s—but this is pure wishful thinking for now, as the performers have already been confirmed: Usher, Reba McEntire, Post Malone, Andra Day, and Tiësto.
