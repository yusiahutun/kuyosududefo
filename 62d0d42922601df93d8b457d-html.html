Boxing: Julio Cesar Chavez: How much did he earn in his main fights?

Publish date: 2024-08-02

Julio Cesar Chavez is one of the boxers that generated the most money during his career, it cannot be compared to the purse that is paid today, but undoubtedly the Mexican did not do badly at all.

Chavez's fortune is very high, with what he earned in boxing he bought houses and sports cars and he has a classic collection in Culiacan, Sinaloa, Mexico.

What were Chavez's biggest purses during his career?

The specialized boxing website izquierdazo.com made a count of Chavez's fights and how much he won in each one of them, here we are going to present the main ones.

1984

Mario "Azabache" Martinez: 30 thousand dollars. His first world championship fight.

1985

Roger Mayweather I: $100,000

1987 Edwin Rosario: 425 thousand dollars

1989

Roger Mayweather II: $750,000

1990

Meldrick Taylor: 1.2 million dollars

1992

Héctor "Macho" Camacho: $3 million dollars official, $5 million unofficial

1993

Greg Haugen: $3 million

Pernell Whitaker: $5 million

1994

Frankie Randall I: $5 million

Frankie Randall II: US$5 million

Meldrick Taylor II: US$5 million

1996

Oscar de la Hoya I: $9 million guaranteed + $6 million (35% of pay-per-view) The biggest purse of his entire career.

1998

Miguel Angel Gonzalez: $3 million

Oscar de la Hoya II: $6 million dollars

2000

Kostya Tszyu: $1.2 million
