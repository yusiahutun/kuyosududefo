Damian Lewis, a British actor, best known for his role an American in HBO miniseries ‘Band of Brothers’ as the U.S. Army Major Richard Winters and as the U.S. Marine Sergeant Nicholas Brody in the Showtime's ‘Homeland.’

The talented actor with three decades of experience is still making the waves through his excellent performances; currently through Showtimes's Billions as a Billionaire Bobby Axelrod. 

That being said, how has the fame treated the star? More on his impressive career and personal life below:

Damian Lewis Bio

Born to Charlotte Mary and John Watcyn Lewis on 11 February 1971, Damian Lewis spent his childhood in St John's Wood, London, England. For siblings, he has two brothers Gareth, William, and a sister named Amanda.

Damian studied at Ashdown House School and later moved on to Guildhall School of Music and Drama.

The actor stands tall with height: 6 feet and an inch (1.85 m).

Damian's Personal Life; Wife & Children

Lewis’s first public relationship was with Katie Razzall, a Channel 4 producer. Damian parted way with his girlfriend as their long-distance relationship became hard to sustain. Shortly after Damian, Lewis dated actress Kristin Davis for a short time.

After two of his previous relationships failed, Damian, struck gold third time around. He married actress Helen McCrory on 4th of July, 2007. Since their union, there have not been any rumors of affairs or anything that could separate the couple.

WIth his wife, Damian parents two children, daughter, Manon, and a son, Gulliver. Currently, Damian lives with his family in a Victorian townhouse in North London.

Career, TV Shows, Net Worth

Before stepping in the professional acting roles in television and films, he served as a telemarketer and a stage actor with the Royal Shakespeare Company. 

Damian first appearance came from an American drama 'Robinson Crusoe' in 1997. The same year, he played Laertes in Jonathan Kent's 'Hamlet,' which brought him to the attention of Steven Spielberg, who cast him as Richard Winters in 'Band of Brothers.'

He then continued to bag roles in 'Keane' and 'Brides' in 2004, 'The Baker' and 'The Escapist' in 2008 and 'Stolen' in 2011.

2011 also had Damian playing the role of Nicholas Brody in 'Homeland.' He then got featured in 'Romeo & Juliet' in 2013, based on the Shakespearean play of the same name. Three years later, he starred in the spy film 'Our Kind of Traitor.'

For his role of Henry VIII in ‘Wolf Hall,’ Damian earned a Golden Globe nomination as well as a Primetime Emmy Award nomination.
