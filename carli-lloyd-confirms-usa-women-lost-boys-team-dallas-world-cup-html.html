Carli Lloyd CONFIRMS USA women once lost 5-2 to an Under 15s boys team - two years BEFORE winning th

Publish date: 2024-10-07

Carli Lloyd has admitted that her two-time World Cup winning USA team was once beaten 5-2 by an FC Dallas Under-15 boys side in a practice game.

The story, while reported on at the time in April 2017, has become something of a legend since with some fans questioning if it is true.

And one of those posed that query to Lloyd herself on X, asking her: 'if it's true you lost to a bunch of 15 year old boys?'.


To Lloyd's credit, she answered something that would have been easier to ignore.

'Yes it’s true. I know…thousands of people have already brought that up,' she said.

Carli Lloyd has admitted the USA World Cup winning women lost to an Under 15 boys team

She replied to a question on X to confirm it was true and that it happened more than once 

Lloyd (top left) posed with the Dallas Under 15 players after their defeat in 2017, as did other stars of the US women's national team 

'They were good. We actually lost to a youth Bayern Munich team in my career as well but then we went on to win Olympics and World Cups. So…..'

She then added in a separate message to another follower: 'They should beat us. Bigger, stronger, faster! Boys always gave us a run for our money! It was great prep.'

The scoreline that day was 5-2 to FC Dallas but the USA squad did not let the defeat dampen their spirits too much.

They still posed for pictures with a number of the Dallas players - both Lloyd herself and Megan Rapinoe were seen having snaps taken with the teenagers as they celebrated their big moment.

And Lloyd was right in her answer, as well, that it didn't derail any success the US team was aiming for.

Two years later, Lloyd and Rapinoe helped the US to a second straight World Cup triumph in the tournament in France.

When Lloyd retired from soccer in 2021, she stepped away from the international scene with an incredible 361 caps and 134 goals for her country, as well as two World Cup wins to her name.
