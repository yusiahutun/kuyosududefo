Where Does Sylvester Stallone Live? Photos of Beverly Hills Home

Publish date: 2024-07-14

Sylvester Stallone was looking to make a pretty penny on his longtime Beverly Hills mega-mansion. The Rocky legend relisted his sprawling home for $85 million in August 2021. Along with his estranged wife, Jennifer Flavin, and their daughters, Sylvester had resided in the beautiful abode since the late 1990s, until he sold it for $58 million in February 2022, according to the Los Angeles Times.

$85 million may sound like a huge asking price, but the Hollywood icon actually listed the residence for much more when he initially put it on the market earlier this year. Per Daily Mail, Sly was trying to sell the residence, which is located in the exclusive North Beverly Park neighborhood, for $110 million in January 2021.

Sitting on a total of three-and-a-half acres, the Rambo star’s old pad boasted 21,000 square feet and came with a total of eight bedrooms, as well as nine full bathrooms and three half-bathrooms.

In addition to their multiple luxurious living areas and fancy dining rooms, Sly and Jennifer’s abode was fitted with amenities, such as a full chef’s kitchen, a bar and cigar room, a movie theater, an art studio and a private gym. The outdoors, which offered gorgeous views of famous Rodeo Drive and downtown Los Angeles, was just as eye-catching.

In the backyard, the former lovebirds — who wed in 1997 and share their girls, Sophia, Sistine and Scarlet — had their very own infinity pool with a spa, as well as a huge patio area where they had the chance to relax and spend some quality time. The Expendables alum was also able to head over to his putting green or work on his luxury vehicles in his eight-car garage.

Although they seemingly had a strong relationship spanning two decades, Jennifer filed for divorce from the Creed star in August 2022, Closer Weekly exclusively confirmed.

Seven months before Sly relisted his California home in 2021, he purchased a new home in Palm Beach, Florida, in December 2020. The Golden Globe winner put down roots in the Sunshine State, buying a 13,241-square-foot property for $35.375M, according to the Palm Beach Daily News.

The Palm Beach pad is a little smaller than his former home in Beverly Hills, sitting on a total of one-and-a-half acres, but it features seven bedrooms, 10 full bathrooms and two half-bathrooms. Talk about living a life of luxury!
