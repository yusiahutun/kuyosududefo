Where is Christopher Tapp now after getting acquitted of Angie Dodge's murder?

Publish date: 2024-05-26

Christopher Tapp's case became a symbol of wrongful convictions in the United States, highlighting the flaws in the justice system and the power of DNA evidence in seeking the truth. Tapp's story has inspired numerous documentaries, books, and legal reforms, making him a significant figure in the fight for justice and prison reform. But where is Christopher Tapp now?

Who is Christopher Tapp?

Christopher Conley Tapp was born in Idaho Falls, Idaho, on July 11, 1976. His parents were Vera Tapp and Payton Conley Tapp Jr. He had one sibling, Ruth Mary Tapp. He attended Skyline High School and enjoyed playing basketball, baseball, and wrestling.

On May 22, 2019, Christopher Tapp married Mary Tapp in Idaho Falls, ID. Stacy and Christopher made their home in Rigby, Idaho. She enjoyed camping, hunting, refurbishing old furniture, spending time with her family, and making new friends everywhere.

Mary Tapp later died on August 14, 2023, due to injuries sustained in an automobile accident.

The murder of Angie Dodge

In June 1996, 18-year-old Angie Dodge returned from her parent's home; she was tragically sexually abused and stabbed to death in her apartment.

Her body was found by her co-workers, who went to check up on her. The suspect remained unknown until early 1997 when Benjamin Hobbs, a friend of Christopher Tapp, was arrested for sexual assault in Nevada.

Christopher Tapp's involvement

Christopher Tapp, a 20-year-old acquaintance of Dodge and Hobbs, was subsequently interviewed by the police multiple times. Initially, Tapp claimed neither he nor Hobbs were involved in the murder.

However, according to Idaho Statesman, his story changed over several interviews, eventually leading to his arrest on charges of being an accessory to a felony.

Despite inconsistencies in Tapp's statements and DNA evidence excluding him and Hobbs from the crime scene, he continued to implicate various individuals, causing his immunity agreements with the prosecution to be voided due to untruthfulness.

Tapp's final confession on Angie's death

In his final statement, Tapp claimed he had been coerced into participating in the crime by Hobbs under threat of death. Despite inconsistencies in his accounts and lack of DNA evidence linking him to the crime, Tapp was eventually convicted.

His trial began on May 12, 1998, in Idaho's Seventh Judicial District Court. His confession, which his attorney argued was coerced, was used against him, leading to his conviction on all charges on May 28, 1998.

Under pressure, Tapp's involvement increased, leading him to confess to participating in the crime. This confession was later disputed by evidence showing that Tapp had not mentioned details of the crime until after seeing the crime scene photos.

The testimony of Destiny Osborne, who claimed to have overheard Tapp and Hobbs discussing the crime at a party, was used against him. He was sentenced to life in prison, with a minimum sentence of 30 years and 10 years for the murder and sexual abuse convictions, respectively.

Tapp fought his conviction through a series of appeals, each of which was rejected. However, in 2007, the Idaho Innocence Project took on his case, requesting DNA testing on hairs recovered from the crime scene. This event eventually led to his dismissal, highlighting the importance of DNA evidence in ensuring justice.

The DNA match

In May 2019, Parabon identified a potential relative of the unknown DNA contributor. An investigator tracked down that person and identified a potential suspect: Brian Leigh Dripps Sr., who lived across the street from Dodge in 1996.

Dripps was living in Caldwell, Idaho, and in May 2019, police collected a cigarette that he had thrown away. The DNA on the cigarette matched the DNA at the crime scene.

Using genetic genealogy technology, Parabon was able to build a family tree from profiles in their database. They obtained DNA samples from six men who matched the profile and were of the right age, living around Idaho Falls at the time of the murder.

However, none were a match. This method identified the seventh person of interest, Brian Dripps, who had been an early suspect but was later overlooked. Dripps's discarded cigarette provided the DNA match to the sample from the crime scene.

In an interview with police, Dripps admitted that he had killed Dodge. He said he had intended to sexually abuse her, not kill her, and that he acted alone. He was charged with sexual abuse and murder. After being questioned, he confessed to the murder and sexual assault, claiming he acted alone and did not know Tapp.

When was Christopher Tapp released?

In July 2019, Tapp's attorneys filed a motion to dismiss the murder charge, saying that the confession had been coerced and that new DNA evidence pointed to Dripps as the sole perpetrator. On July 17, 2019, the murder charge was dismissed. Tapp was officially exonerated.

What happened to Chris Tapp after his release?

Tapp later filed a lawsuit seeking damages from the city of Idaho Falls. At the same time, District Attorney Clark, who previously claimed Tapp was guilty, supported the motion to vacate the murder conviction.

According to the New York Times, in 2022, the Idaho City settled, approving Christopher Tapp's settlement of $11.7 million. The mayor formally apologized for the harm and damages done to Tapp.

Where is Christopher Tapp now?

After his release, he started settling into a new life. He started working at a bag-making factory, bought a home, married, and saw old friends.

He lobbied Idaho's governor to sign a bill providing compensation to the wrongfully convicted, and in 2021, that bill became law, providing $62,000 for each year a person was incarcerated and $75,000 annually for people who had been on death row.

Christopher Tapp's accident and death

According to NBC News, Tapp died on November 23, 2023, after he was rushed to a Nevada hospital from his Resorts World suite in Las Vegas. His death was initially described as an accident, the Las Vegas Metropolitan Police Department stated, but the Clark County Coroner's Office later ruled it a murder.

Christopher Tapp's funeral was held at Coltrin Mortuary in Idaho Falls. The service was live-streamed on EastIdahoNews and YouTube.
