Who is Magic Johnson?

Born on August 14, 1959, in Lansing, Michigan, Earvin "Magic" Johnson Jr. is an American entrepreneur and a former professional basketball player, frequently hailed as the greatest point guard in the sport's history. The strong work ethic of his parents, Earvin Sr. and Christine Johnson, profoundly influenced him, shaping the person and the professional he would become.

Johnson attended Everett High School and then Michigan State College, where he played basketball under coach Jud Heathcote.

Magic Johnson's wife, Cookie Johnson, is a devoted philanthropist and works as the board of directors' secretary for the Magic Johnson Foundation. Johnson has three children: son Andre with ex-girlfriend Melissa Mitchell, son EJ, and daughter Elisa with wife Cookie.

Magic Johnson’s basketball career

At 15, Earvin Johnson got the nickname "Magic" following an impressive triple-double performance that included 36 points, 18 rebounds, and 16 assists during his sophomore year at Everett High School.

In a press conference held on November 7, 1991, Johnson announced his HIV status; he was at the peak of his NBA fame. He said;

"Because of the HIV that I have, I will retire from the Lakers today… I just want to say that I'm going to miss playing, and I will now become a spokesman for the HIV virus.”

Magic Johnson was diagnosed with HIV after taking a medical test for a life insurance policy. He acquired the virus through unprotected sexual encounters with multiple women.

Now 64, Johnson runs the Magic Johnson Foundation, which funds HIV education and prevention programs in the USA's most vulnerable neighbourhoods.
