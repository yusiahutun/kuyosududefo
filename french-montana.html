Career and Professional Life

Talking about his career, In 2002, when he was still a teenager, French started his career as a combat rapper. He and a close friend produced a line of street DVDs dubbed “Cocaine City” that included interviews with well-known rappers as well as up-and-coming artists. Street music was another key topic.

He also showed his musical prowess on the DVDs. The eight-year run of the show was made possible by the assistance of several childhood friends. In 2007, he released his debut mixtape, “French Revolution.” Rappers Uncle Murda, Jae Millz, and Tony Yayo were also involved in the collaborations. He released his second mixtape the following year under the title “Live from Africa.”

He worked with rapper Max B that same year to create the mixtape “Coke Wave,” which was released in February 2009. Over the following months, he kept producing and releasing other mixtapes. In September 2010, he released his eleventh mixtape, titled “Coke Boys.” Both on the radio and in clubs, the song “Choppa Choppa Down” became a hit.
