To learn more about this unique hairstyle, here are the ten best Zig Zag all-back hairstyles for your natural hair.

1. Simple Zig Zag stitch braids

Simple Zig Zag stitch braids are a distinctive hairstyle characterised by medium, tightly woven braids that follow a zigzag parting. A bun is used to hold the braids in place. This hairdo combines perfectly to create a look perfect for work or play.

2. Zig Zag cornrows hairstyles

Zig Zag cornrows are unique and stylish hairstyles. This fun design is one to go for if you want a look suitable for the weekend or holiday. The style involves braiding the hair in a zigzag pattern across the scalp with a simple or complex pattern as desired. This design applies to both men and women.

3. Zig Zag box braids

Zig Zag box braids involve creating box braids in a zigzag pattern across the scalp. The box braid version is ideal for creative and fun Zig Zag all-back hairstyles. Though it has a complicated appearance, this design is achievable in a simple or intricate pattern.

If you want Zig Zag all-back hairstyles with beads, this short Zig Zag plait back with beads is an ideal pick. Creative and distinct, the hairstyle features small, tightly woven plaits in a zigzag pattern across the scalp. Adding beads at the ends of the plaits adds a unique touch to the style.

5. Zig Zag parts straight back braids

Zig Zag parts straight back braids are a distinctive hairstyle characterised by straight back braids with parts in a zigzag pattern across the scalp. 10. Stitch braids with Zig Zag parting

As the name suggests, stitch braids with zigzag parts feature thick stitch braids with separating parts in a zigzag pattern. This creative hairstyle is versatile and suitable for casual days out or formal events.

How to plait zigzag style

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to plait your hair in a zigzag style:

  • Part your hair: Begin by parting your hair in half from ear to ear to create two sections of hair: one at the front and one at the back.
  • Section the front: Divide the front section of your hair into three segments.
  • Start braiding: Choose one side and braid a small segment of hair towards the middle. Stop once you reach the middle.
  • Alternate sides: Take a small segment of hair from the middle section and start braiding it towards the other side. Stop once you reach the other side.
  • Repeat the process: Continue alternating sides and braiding small hair segments until you reach the end of the front section. Make sure to keep your fingers close to the scalp as you braid.
  • Secure the braids: After braiding the front section, braid the excess hair and secure it with a hairband or clip.
