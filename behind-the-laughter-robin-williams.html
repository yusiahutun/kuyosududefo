Behind the Laughter: Robin Williams

Publish date: 2024-03-09

Robin Williams, the comedic genius and beloved actor, left an indelible mark on the world of entertainment. Beyond his laughter-inducing performances, Williams used his platform to advocate for mental health awareness. His journey, marked by struggles with mental health and addiction, provides profound lessons for us all.

In this essay, we will delve into the advocacy and legacy of Robin Williams, focusing primarily on his message regarding mental health Robin Williams’ life and struggles with mental health offer valuable lessons, highlighting the importance of compassion and empathy. His suicide serves as a poignant reminder that depression is a relentless adversary.

Brief Introduction to Robin Williams

Robin Williams was a renowned comedian who was beloved in the entertainment industry and a four-time Oscar nominee. While suicide was labelled as the primary cause of his death, as per the autopsy reports, the final years of his life were surrounded by many personal and mental health challenges.

From dealing with the guilt that comes with divorce to having to accept the stalling of his film career to adjusting to a new life with his third wife, these challenges were daunting for William. And this was not it. In 2014, doctors diagnosed him with Parkinson’s, a neurodegenerative disorder that affects movement. It was a cataclysmic diagnosis for this actor. Sadly, this was a misdiagnosis. After his death, doctors found that Robin Williams had Lewy Body Dementia, an aggressive brain disorder. Moreover, people commonly associate this disorder with the risk of suicide.

You might know him as John Keating from Dead Poets Society, as the voice actor of Genie from Aladdin, as Sean Maguire from the American psychological movie Good Will Hunting. Robin Williams has worked for phenomenal movies and TV shows. After his death, his co-workers and actors had to say a lot about him. Comedians spoke about how no one thought faster on stage than Williams; those who made movies with him said he never did the same take twice, always ad-libbing and getting funnier each time (Freeman, 2021). Others talked more confidently stating that he was depressed or had succumbed to old addictions (The Guardian, 2021).

While his remarkable career had slowed down, admittingly leading to financial troubles as well, Robin Williams did not stop working. Till the final years of his life, despite the notorious disease affecting him daily and despite the overwhelming uncertainty of a good future looming ahead, Robin Williams persevered.

In August 2012, he appeared in an episode of Louie. In the same year, he was filming the movie The Angriest Man in Brooklyn, a morbid indie comedy. He also appeared in The Crazy Ones, a CBS comedy show released in 2013. The show did not gain a notable audience. It was shredded under the hands of critics. At least some critics gently noted that the Robin of The Crazy Ones was no longer the indefatigable dynamo they had come to adore in an earlier era. Others were not so
diplomatic, like the one who simply wrote, “Williams seems exhausted. So is this show” (Vanity Fair, 2018).

Battle with Addiction

Williams’ struggles with addiction were a well-documented part of his life story. He openly discussed his experiences with substance abuse, including alcohol and cocaine, in interviews and even used humour to address his demons during his stand-up comedy routines. His addiction issues were often intertwined with his struggles with depression and anxiety, creating a complex and challenging landscape for him to navigate. Allen Stephan, a close friend of Williams said that Robin was highly addicted to cocaine and that he couldn’t perform without it (The Economic Times, 2023).

Howie Mandel, a fellow comedian, revealed that Williams began incorporating addiction-related jokes into his comedic stand-up jokes. In a sense, he was making light of his addiction. While audiences laughed, there was a grim reminder of his increasing addiction. The life of Robin Williams served as a monument to the ongoing struggle that many people have with addiction. His narrative serves as a reminder that people with personal issues can exist alongside individuals who brighten the lives of many others. The disclosures from his pals shed light on the difficult road to rehabilitation and the obstacles Williams overcame throughout his storied career.

The Dark Side of Comedy

While the world did not know the challenges Robin Williams was facing, his close friends, family and wife did. Zachary Pym Williams, Robin Williams’ son, notes how whenever they met him, he seemed in anguish. Susan, his third wife, witnessed Robin’s health deteriorating while she was at home. Robin would writhe around the bed as they tried to go to sleep at night, or more often than not, he would be up and eager to discuss whatever new fantasy his imagination had created. In an attempt to take control of the illness, Robin tried a variety of therapies. He kept going to therapy, worked out with a personal trainer, rode his bike, and even located a Stanford University specialist who taught him self-hypnosis. But there was a limit to what these tactics could accomplish.

Cheri Minns, his makeup artist, notes how his illness and motor impairments continued to worsen. He was losing weight drastically, he had trouble remembering his likes and his memory was failing him. Minns adds, “He was sobbing in my arms at the end of every day. It was horrible. Horrible. But I just didn’t know” (Itskoff, 2018).

At last, Robin received an explanation for the complex web of illnesses that had been afflicting him. Being informed that he had Parkinson’s, a disease that would gradually take away his abilities every day, hollowing him out and leaving behind a diminished shell of a person, was one of Robin’s deepest-seated and longest-standing fears. On the night of August 10, 2014, Susan noted how she felt Williams was getting better. She felt the medication was working and he was finally getting sleep. The next morning, Robin’s bedroom door was still closed and Susan was happy to see him rest. However, hours passed and when there was no response from Williams, the bedroom door was pried open forcefully. A horrible discovery was made. Robin had hanged himself and was dead.

Conclusion

To the world, Robin Williams was the epitome of laughter. Robin Williams’ story serves as a stark reminder that depression does not discriminate. It affects people of all backgrounds, regardless of their age, gender, profession, or social status. Williams’ extraordinary success did not shield him from the clutches of this relentless mental health condition. His struggles with substance abuse, degenerative health and anxiety further underscore that depression often accompanies other challenges, making it a complex and multifaceted battle.
