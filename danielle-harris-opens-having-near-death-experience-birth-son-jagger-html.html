Danielle Harris opens up about having a 'near death experience' during the birth of her son Jagger

Publish date: 2024-05-28

Actress Danielle Harris is opening up about the scary complications she had during the birth of son Jagger.

The Don't Tell Mom The Babysitter's Dead actress, 41, got candid about her 'traumatic birth experience' in an Instagram birth announcement posted almost a month after her baby's birth.

The Halloween franchise actress began with a sweet message to her baby before telling her harrowing story about her 'near death experience' during her delivery.

Scary stuff: Horror film regular Danielle Harris opened up about her harrowing birth experience with her second child, sharing her experience in a lengthy Instagram post Monday

'Introducing...Jagger Maxwell Gross Born 10/8/2018 !!!!' the mother-of-two began.

'Mommy and Daddy are so lucky to be able to add you to our family. We are a fierce 4some now and can’t wait to watch you grow and live this life as our baby boy and little brother to the BEST big brother a boy could ask for!!'

The Wild Thornberrys' voice actress then explained why she delayed her baby announcement and updated followers about her postpartum journey.

'Giving birth is the most dangerous thing a woman can do in her lifetime...after my traumatic birth experience this time around, it took me a while to share a photo with the world.'

Rattled from the hospital experience, Danielle said: 'Time was really needed to clear my head and bond with my baby just he and I in our own private little bubble.'

Traumatic: 'After having a horrible eclampsia seizure on the operating table when my blood pressure bottomed and I blacked out, I saw moments of my life flashing before me in fast forward and I missed the birth of my baby'

She then described the 'traumatizing' experience that was Jagger's birth.

'After having a horrible eclampsia seizure on the operating table when my blood pressure bottomed and I blacked out, I saw moments of my life flashing before me in fast forward and I missed the birth of my baby.'

Eclampsia seizures occur in women suffering from pre-eclampia, a condition that characterized by high blood pressure in expecting women.

'Needless to say that was the closest to a near death experience as I’d ever come in real life.'

She thanked her higher power, saying: 'I am forever grateful and blessed whether it be G-d or something/someone else (thank you Gram, Dad, Thor) that a higher power stepped in that day and intervened what could have been the end of the road for me.'

Little guy: Harris is also mom to one-and-a-half-year-old Carter, above

Getting real, she said: 'Dying during childbirth was definitely not on my list of ways I thought I would leave this world and I’m glad to cross that possibility off my list. Women are warriors and I’m looking forward to being around many more years soaking up all these precious moments with my baby boys and family. 

Though the experience was terrifying, she revealed how it made her count her blessings. 

'Life is short. You never know when your number is gonna be up, so take time to enjoy what matters most. Love one another and love yourself because the world wouldn’t be the same without you...'

Harris is also mom to one-and-a-half-year-old Carter. She conceived both children Lthrough in vitro fertilization, with hubby of five-years David Gross.
