Haleigh Cox who helped change Georgia's medical marijuana law loses home in fire

Publish date: 2024-04-07

The family of a six-year-old girl who's health problems changed marijuana laws in Georgia have lost all of their belongings to a house fire.

The home of Haleigh Cox, the namesake for Haleigh's Hope Act, burned down on Tuesday at about 4pm, just days before she and her mother, Janea Cox, were returning from Colorado.

The bill legalized low-THC cannabis oil for certain medication-resistant epilepsies. Haleigh suffered severe seizures and her mother moved her to Colorado last year to be treated with the drug. 

The home of six-year-old Haleigh Cox, the namesake for Haleigh's Hope Act, burned down on Tuesday at about 4pm

The fire, which officials believe to have been caused by something electrical, started while Haleigh's father, Brian Cox, was mowing the lawn

Haleigh and her mother Janea Cox, who have been living in Colorado since last year, were supposed to return to Georgia on August 3

Haleigh has a a seizure disorder that once caused her more than 100 seizures a day, but cannabis oil has helped reduce her convulsions. 

Janea Cox said on her Facebook page that her husband, Brian Cox, who still lives in Colorado, called on Thursday to tell her that their house had burned down.
