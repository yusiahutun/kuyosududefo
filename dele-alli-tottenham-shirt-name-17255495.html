Why does Dele Alli wear Dele on the back of his Tottenham shirt? The real reason explained

Publish date: 2024-02-22

So here is the answer.

Alli decided to get Dele on his match shirts due to feeling like had had no connection with the surname.

He explained: "I wanted a name on my shirt that represented who I am.

“I feel I have no connection with the Alli surname.

"This is not a decision I have taken without a lot of thought and discussion with family close to me."

It’s all down to his upbringing.

Alli’s mum Denise described in January how she gave her son away aged 13 as she battled an alcohol addiction. She faced having her children removed by Social Services.

Denise told The Sun on Sunday: “I had to let him go to give him a better future.

“Emotionally, it was heartbreaking but it was the right thing to do."

Alli was handed over to a couple, Alan and Sally Hickford, who he is said to call his adoptive parents.

For his whole career, growing up at MK Dons and after moving to Spurs, it was always Alli.

Even at the Euros in the summer, his surname and No.20 was on the back of his England strip.

But he made the change just before the 2016-17 season.

Tottenham announced the reasons on their official club website.
