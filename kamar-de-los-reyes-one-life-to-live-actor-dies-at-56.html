Kamar de los Reyes, 'One Life To Live' Actor, Dies at 56

Publish date: 0001-01-01

Kamar de los Reyes, a Puerto Rican actor renowned for portraying Antonio Vega on the soap opera One Life to Live, passed away on December 24, 2023, at the age of 56. He was known for his multifaceted talents as a singer, dancer, and voice actor, and he graced the screens of various films, TV shows, and video games. Kamar was married to actress Sherri Saum and was the proud father of twin sons. His family, friends, and fans fondly remember him as a gifted, charismatic, and compassionate individual.

Inside His Early Life 

Kamar de los Reyes was born in San Juan, Puerto Rico, on November 8, 1967. His upbringing took place in the backstage wings of Las Vegas hotels, as his father, a drummer and percussionist from Cuba, and his mother, a dancer from Puerto Rico, raised him. Additionally, both of his older brothers, Daniel and Walfredo Jr., also pursued careers as musicians.

Kamar de los Reyes was a famous Puerto Rican actor. 

At the age of 8, de los Reyes uncovered his passion for performing when he danced with Lola Falana on stage. His pursuit of singing and dancing continued through high school, and he made his acting debut as El Gallo in The Fantastiks. Following this, he relocated to Los Angeles in 1988, securing a role in the film Salsa and the TV series Dirty Dancing.

The Remarkable Career of Kamar de los Reyes

In 1994, de los Reyes gained widespread recognition for his portrayal of Pedro Quinn, a gay Irish-Mexican boxer, in the Off-Broadway play Blade to the Heat. The New York Times lauded his performance, describing him as the most captivating presence in the production.

Additionally, he starred alongside Patrick Stewart as Ferdinand in a 1995 rendition of The Tempest.

Kamar de los Reyes is renowned for his portrayal of Antonio Vega on the soap opera One Life to Live.

In 1997, de los Reyes became a cast member of the ABC soap opera One Life to Live, portraying Antonio Vega, a police officer and a member of a notable Latino family. His portrayal made him a fan favorite and a symbol of attraction, leading to his inclusion in People Magazine’s “Fabulous 50” list.

Although he departed the show in 2009, he made a return for its concluding episodes in 2012.

De los Reyes has been featured in a variety of movies and TV shows, including Nixon, The Way She Moves, Love and Suicide, Salt, Sleepy Hollow, The Gifted, and The Rookie.

Additionally, he lent his voice and performed motion capture for the character Raul Menendez, the primary antagonist in the video game Call of Duty: Black Ops II, and its sequels.

Take A Look AT His Personal Life

De los Reyes and actress Sherri Saum tied the knot in 2007 after meeting on the set of One Life to Live. In 2014, they welcomed twin sons, Michael Luis and John Rubén.

The late actor Kamar de los Reyes and his wife Sherri Saum. Image Source: New York Post

Additionally, the couple established their own production company, MaPá Productions, and collaborated on their first short film, First Strike Butcher Knife, in 2017, showcasing their talents both in front of and behind the camera.

What was the Causes of De los Reyes' Death?

De los Reyes passed away on December 24, 2023, at 56 after battling cancer.  His death was announced by his publicist, who said he was surrounded by his family and friends. He is fondly remembered by colleagues and fans as a talented, charismatic, and kind-hearted individual.
