Sensei Institute of Technology courses offered, fees, and requirements in 2024/2025

Publish date: 2024-05-29

Sensei Institute of Technology equips students with skills that will guarantee a secure future. The institute has two campuses, one in Nairobi and another in Nakuru. Sensei Institute courses include diplomas, certificates, artisan, craft, and other technical programs.

TABLE OF CONTENTS

Sensei Institute aims to develop a strong pool of skilled and market-ready trainees who meet the country's labor needs. As a result, the school has enabled many Kenyans to acquire skills for self-employment and entrepreneurship.

Sensei Institute courses, fees, and requirements

Sensei Institute's technical courses and non-technical programs empower and train competent youth in technical skills through a professional, industry-based, practical training approach. Below is a list of Sensei Institute of Technology courses:

Technical courses

  • Mechanics
  • Agricultural mechanics
  • Plant Operator
  • Construction
  • Electrical/Electronics

Non-technical courses

  • Driving
  • Hairdressing and Beauty
  • Sound & Media

  • Safety Courses
  • Hospitality

Computing courses

  • Computers & ICT
  • Computer Technician

Most courses offered at Sensei last between 1 to 6 months. Intake is in the first week of every month, and boarders should carry blankets, bed sheets, toiletries, buckets, plates, cups, spoons, etc. Below is the Sensei Institute of Technology fees structure for all courses the school offers. The fee stated applies to Kenyan citizens. Non-citizens are required to pay an extra 20% of the fee charged.

Is Sensei a government institution?

Sensei Institute of Technology is a private technical and vocational training center (TVC) registered by the government of Kenya through bodies like the Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET), the National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA), and the National Industrial Training Authority (NITA).
