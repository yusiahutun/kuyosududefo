Fans go wild for Zach Bryan's mugshot and vow to print it out on T-shirts after country music star,

Publish date: 2024-09-07

Fans have clamored for country singer Zach Bryan following the release of his mugshot after he was arrested in Vinita, Oklahoma, on Thursday over a clash with highway cops.

The 27-year-old artist was held in custody in the Craig County Jail in connection with the obstruction of an investigation, tweeting on Thursday that he had 'had an incident with the Oklahoma Highway Patrol. Emotions got the best of me and I was out of line in the things I said.'

He said at the time: 'I support law enforcement as much as anyone can, I was just frustrated in the moment, it was unlike me and I apologize. They brought me to jail, and there is a mug shot of me floating around.'

Since then, a number of accounts have emerged claiming to offer the mugshot printed on T-shirts as unofficial merchandise. Users have said the singer's arrest has made him 'MORE attractive'.

One went so far as to write: 'I know I'm toxic because Zach Bryan's mugshot makes me love him even more'. 

Country singer Zach Bryan, 27, was arrested Thursday in Vinita, Oklahoma in connection with obstruction of investigation

The singer-songwriter, who is a veteran of the Navy, addressed details of the incident in a tweet on Thursday evening 

Fans quickly took to Twitter to share their thoughts on the mugshot circulating online 

The Something in the Orange performer has posted a few more tweets since addressing the incident.

He indicated was traveling to watch his favorite football team - the Philadelphia Eagles - in their season opener against the New England Patriots in Massachusetts on Sunday. 

When a fan asked him how his dog was doing after the incident, Bryan said the canine was 'perfect' as he was 'fed and snoozing and riding shotgun.'

He also wrote: 'STILL F***N HIGHWAYING ACROSS THE COUNTRY OL SONS LETS GOOOO.'

Users generally expressed their support for Bryan in spite of the confrontation.

One said: 'We still love and are here to support you, Nobody is perfect and things happen. 'You are human just like the rest of us! Stay true Okie son!'

Another wrote: 'We live. We learn. We move on.'

Within hours, fans started talking about the now widely-circulated mugshot, which shows Bryan wearing a blue shirt with a sporting a moustache.

One X, formerly known as Twitter, user wrote: 'I know I have issues cuz I saw Zach Bryan got arrested and I found him MORE attractive.'

User Austin Basler shared an edited picture of Bryan in an orange jumpsuit and wrote: 'What if Zach Bryan was the something in orange all along?', referring to his song of the same name.

'The haters are real mad because going to jail makes ZB infinitely cooler than he already was, I hope he's in there writing bangers 'about it.' 

User TJ Eckert wrote: 'There's gonna be millions of tshirts [sic] sold with the Zach Bryan mugshot aren't there[?]'

A number of users commented on the mugshot's potential use in merchandizing - and some apparently went so far as to set up online stores.

The singer-songwriter, who is a veteran of the Navy, released his eponymous fourth studio album in August, which premiered in the top spot of the Billboard Hot 100 chart.

Bryan found fame sharing his music to YouTube, with a song about his past service titled Heading South going viral.

He has released four albums in the past four years, beginning with 2019's DeAnn, then 2020's Elisabeth and 2022's American Heartbreak.

He has climbed the country music ranks in recent years, debuting at the famed Grand Ole Opry in Nashville in April of 2021.
