Meet the Experts

  • Serena Mraz, M.D., is a board-certified dermatologist at Solano Dermatology Associates in Vallejo, California.
  • Michael Somenek, M.D., is a double board-certified facial plastic surgeon based in New York City and Washington, D.C.

Are eye creams really safe for sensitive skin?

According to Somenek, people with sensitive skin should be on the hunt for eye creams containing gentle, soothing ingredients, like chamomile, thermal spring water, and niacinamide, which are often labeled as hypoallergenic and are fragrance-free.

How should someone with sensitive skin apply eye cream?

You don't need to use a ton of pressure when applying your eye cream of choice, especially as the area is among the most delicate on your face. “Eye cream should be gently patted on clean dry skin using the fourth finger from the outside of the eye and directed inward toward the nose,” Mraz advises. “This will minimize any pressure or trauma with the application.”
