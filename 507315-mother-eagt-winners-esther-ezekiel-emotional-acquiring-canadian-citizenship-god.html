Mother of EAGT Winners Esther and Ezekiel Emotional After Acquiring Canadian Citizenship: &quot;WeThank

Publish date: 2024-05-26
  • Ugandan Julie Birungu, a resilient mother of three, radiated pure joy as she shared the incredible news
  • Overflowing with gratitude and the duration it took, she thanked God for their family officially becoming Canadian citizens
  • A renowned gospel singer in her homeland, Julie acknowledged that their new status would likely upset some people
  • However, she refused to let their negativity affect her spirit or discourage her from embracing their newfound home in Canada

Driven by an unwavering love for her children, Julie Birungu made the brave decision to relocate to Canada after her divorce, seeking a brighter future for her precious trio.

As a renowned gospel singer in her native country, Birungu left, and finally, her hard work paid off as she and her three children were officially Canadian citizens.

She acknowledged that her happiness might stir mixed emotions in others but remained undeterred by any potential opposition and focused on the significant milestone they had achieved.

Birungu inspiring change

Birungu, the proud mom of Ezekiel and his sister Esther Mutesasira, winners of the esteemed 2019 East Africa's Got Talent (EAGT), embarked on this journey for her children's sake.

Their talents shone brightly, and their remarkable achievement propelled Julie's leap of faith.

After disclosing she was lesbian, the intolerant environment towards the queer community in her native Uganda forced her to seek refuge elsewhere.

Officially Canadians

As she had predicted, people flooded the comment section. Some were proud of her and the sacrifices she made to give her children a better future.

While some said they wouldn't dare to begin again if the risks were that great.

She wrote:

"We thank God we are now officially Canadian citizens; now I know some of you are going to click."
