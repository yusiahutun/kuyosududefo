What is Misogyny Quiz Everyday Habits Women Normalize As Part of Life

Publish date: 2024-09-02

Women have lived with sexism and misogyny every day for centuries. Millennia, even. But as the #MeToo movement continues to topple titans of industry, it’s also important that we make some noise about the everyday misogyny and sexism that may not reach Weinstein levels of assault or skullduggery.

Because it’s not just about asking men not to assault women, or not to tell sexist jokes. It’s about examining society’s own learned assumptions and habits, and challenging them—the daily offenses and indignities that women normalize and accept as “part of life," the often unseen things that keep women down, personally and professionally, while also perpetuating patriarchy.

So here, from my experience in India, the U.S., and all over the world, is a list of the “little” things that make us complicit in misogyny. If you answer yes to any of the below questions, that makes you complicit. It's ok—you can be honest. Then let’s tear them down, together.

Sohini Bhattacharya is the CEO of Breakthrough, a global human rights organization working to make violence and discrimination against women unacceptable. Before joining Breakthrough, Sohini spent 10 years at Ashoka Innovators for the Public, an international organisation building an ecosystem for social entrepreneurs, first ramping up the India program and then working on institution building for Ashoka in South Asia. Sohini also worked as the India Strategy consultant at the Asian Venture Philanthropy Network. Sohini is a founding trustee of Read India, and a board member of Dastkar, a society for indigenous crafts and craftspeople. She also serves on the board of Kolkata Sanved, which uses dance movement therapy to recover from trauma and violence and Aakar Social Ventures, an organisation working on menstrual hygiene in India and Africa.
